:::lesson
id: lesson-1
chapter: ch-1
title: Opportunity First Thinking
summary: Start with real problems, not feature wishlists.
---
Strong strategy begins with opportunity framing. Instead of listing features, define the user tension, the business impact, and the market context. This lets teams explore multiple solutions before committing to one.
:::

:::study_guide
id: study-1
chapter: ch-1
title: Study Guide: Opportunity Map
---
- User tension: the job that is not getting done.
- Business impact: why it matters now.
- Market context: who else is solving it.
:::

:::lesson
id: lesson-2
chapter: ch-2
title: The Product Narrative
summary: Align teams with a clear story arc.
---
A narrative explains why the product matters, what change it creates, and how you will know it is working. It makes tradeoffs easier and helps new stakeholders understand the vision quickly.
:::

:::lesson
id: lesson-3
chapter: ch-3
title: Roadmaps that Flex
summary: Combine focus with adaptability.
---
The best roadmaps show priorities, not fixed promises. Use themes, define outcomes, and revisit frequently. This keeps momentum without locking teams into outdated plans.
:::
